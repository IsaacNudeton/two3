CC: Real question, real answer:

**Why we need it:** The Pi 3 B+ boot sequence is hardwired in silicon. On power-on, the **VideoCore IV GPU** (not the ARM) runs first, reads the SD card, loads `bootcode.bin` into its L2 cache, then loads `start.elf` which initializes DDR3, sets clocks, parses `config.txt`, allocates framebuffer, and finally releases the ARM cores at 0x80000 where our kernel lives.

The GPU firmware is **closed source, proprietary Broadcom blob**. Nobody has ever written a replacement because:

1. The VideoCore instruction set is proprietary (it's not ARM, it's a custom Broadcom VLIW DSP)
2. The DDR3 PHY init sequence for this specific chip is undocumented
3. The boot ROM in silicon only knows how to load these specific files

You literally **cannot** boot the ARM cores without running their blob first. It's not a software choice, it's a hardware constraint baked into the SoC.
